What this dashboard pulls

Core water feeds

  • Currituck Sound level: USGS station 02043433, instantaneous gage height on the east bank at Corolla, shown with 1, 7, and 30 day chart windows.
  • Duck FRF surf buoy: NOAA NDBC station 44056 realtime observations for wave height, recent water temperature history, and air temperature; nearby Carova/NWS weather fills wind, pressure, and dew point when the buoy leaves those fields blank.
  • Swell details: NOAA NDBC spectral feed for 44056, used for dominant swell and wind-wave breakdown when the spectral file is fresh.
  • Atlantic tides: NOAA CO-OPS high/low predictions for Sandbridge, VA station 8639428, used because it is the closest tide-prediction station currently modeled for the VA/NC line oceanfront.

Map and weather feeds

  • Buoy map: NOAA NDBC realtime station files for Cape Henry, Virginia Beach offshore, OOI Pioneer moorings, Duck FRF, Duck FRF 26m, and Nags Head.
  • Station freshness: The map counts buoys with recent usable water-temperature observations and labels stale readings so they are not mistaken for current conditions.
  • Fire station weather: Currituck WeatherSTEM Carova Beach public portal data, with NWS point observations and forecast data as fallbacks where needed.
  • Forecast strips: Short hourly and daily summaries are normalized into compact cards so wind, precipitation chance, and temperature are comparable at a glance.

Live cam and caching

  • Surf cam: Corolla Light Resort webcam displayed through the Vista-hosted embed for stream 2O09J4QSEV, with playback, rewind, snapshot, and viewer count controlled by the Vista player.
  • Refresh cadence: Server snapshots are cached briefly to keep the dashboard fast and avoid repeatedly hammering public data providers.
  • Local fallback: The browser keeps the latest valid snapshot in local storage, then clearly marks the dashboard as cached if a refresh fails.
  • Known gaps: Tide predictions are guidance rather than observed water level, buoy observations can lag, and the live cam depends on third-party stream availability.
